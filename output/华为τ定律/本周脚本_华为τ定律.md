## 口播脚本（约430字）

**【Hook】**  
你以为芯片进步，永远都靠“做得更小”吗？华为这次说：**不一定。**摩尔定律这条老路快走到墙边了，现在要换个方向——不是拼命缩小面积，而是**拼命缩短信号跑一圈的时间**。这就是它刚发布的**韬，也就是τ定律**。

**【问题】**  
过去几十年，芯片升级像收纳高手：把同样的东西塞进更小的盒子里，性能就上去了。但问题是，盒子已经快压到极限了。再缩，成本越来越高，难度越来越大，信号在芯片里“跑来跑去”的延迟，反而开始拖后腿。你可以理解成：城市楼越来越密，但路没变短，车照样堵。

**【华为的解法】**  
华为这次的思路很有意思：既然房子没法无限缩小，那就**把路修短**。τ定律盯的不是几何尺寸，而是“时间常数”，说白了就是：让电信号更快到达。它提到一个核心技术叫**逻辑折叠**，你可以把它想成原来电路像摊平的一张大纸，现在把纸折起来，本来很远的两点一下靠近了，走线更短，绕路更少，速度就上来了。

**【实际成果】**  
这事儿也不只是概念。华为说，过去6年，基于这套思路已经设计并量产了**381款芯片**；今年秋天的新**麒麟芯片**，会率先用上逻辑折叠；到2031年，高端芯片还会继续冲性能。

**【CTA】**  
所以你可以这样复述给朋友：**摩尔定律是把芯片做更小，τ定律是让信号跑更快。**你觉得这会是芯片下一阶段的新路吗？评论区聊聊。

# 二、TikTok版口播脚本

## Titles (3 options)
1. Huawei just proposed a post-Moore’s Law idea  
2. What is Huawei’s “Tau Law”? Here’s the simple version  
3. Chips may not need to get much smaller anymore

## TikTok Script（约167 words）

**[Hook]**  
What if the next leap in chips is **not** making them smaller? That’s basically what Huawei just said with its new **Tau Law**.

**[Problem]**  
For decades, Moore’s Law worked like magic: pack more transistors into a smaller space, get better chips. But now that trick is hitting a wall. Shrinking gets insanely expensive, insanely hard, and there’s another problem: even if you cram more stuff in, signals still need to travel across the chip. So it’s like building a denser city, but the roads are still long and traffic still sucks.

**[Huawei’s solution]**  
Huawei’s idea is: stop obsessing only over size, and start shrinking **time**. Specifically, the time it takes signals to move. Their key concept is called **Logic Folding**. Imagine a huge sheet of paper with two points far apart. If you fold the paper, those points get closer instantly. Same idea: shorter paths, less delay, better efficiency.

**[Results]**  
And this isn’t just a slide deck. Huawei says it has already mass-produced **381 chips** based on this path. Its 2026 fall Kirin chip will be the first to use Logic Folding.

**[CTA]**  
So the simple version is: Moore’s Law shrinks size. Tau Law shrinks delay. Smart idea—or real future? Let me know.
