## 🎯 核心论点（一句话）
A lot of tech layoffs are being **presented as “AI-related,”** but Jensen Huang argues that explanation is often too convenient — because the timing of many cuts doesn’t cleanly match when generative AI became truly usable at scale.

## 📊 可用事实要点（按使用优先级排序）

### 必用（经过核实、最硬的事实）
1. Jensen Huang said connecting layoffs to AI is a “lazy” and “irresponsible” narrative. — 出处: CNA, May 25, 2026  
2. Huang said generative AI has only become meaningfully productive at scale in roughly the last six months, suggesting some layoff timelines do not neatly line up with AI deployment. — 出处: CNA, May 25, 2026  
3. Nikkei Asia reported **78,557 tech jobs** were cut globally in **Q1 2026**. — 出处: Nikkei Asia, Apr 8, 2026  
4. Nikkei also reported that AI’s full labor impact may still be ahead, citing Cognizant’s chief AI officer. — 出处: Nikkei Asia, Apr 8, 2026  
5. Huang later said in Taipei: “AI is not the reason we are going to have layoffs. AI is the way we avoid it, so that we could be more successful.” — 出处: Nikkei Asia, May 27, 2026  
6. Nvidia is expanding in Taiwan, with plans presented as growing local headcount to 4,000. — 出处: Nikkei Asia, May 27, 2026  
7. Layoffs.fyi tracks **115,430 tech layoffs in 2026 YTD** across **152 companies**. — 出处: Layoffs.fyi

### 可用（有一定支撑但需谨慎表达）
1. Some companies and executives appear to frame restructuring in AI terms even when the business timeline may be more complicated. — 出处: CNA + Nikkei Asia — ⚠️ 注意: 用 “framed as” / “presented as,” 不要说 “fake” 或 “lied”  
2. The bigger disruption from AI may still be coming, meaning today’s “AI layoff” narrative could be early, incomplete, or strategically presented. — 出处: Nikkei Asia — ⚠️ 注意: 强调这是 “may be yet to come”  
3. Nvidia’s expansion can be used as a contrast: while some firms cut jobs and mention AI, Nvidia publicly presents AI as a growth driver and hiring driver. — 出处: Nikkei Asia — ⚠️ 注意: 不要泛化成 “AI creates more jobs than it destroys”

## 🔄 核心反转/Twist
The twist is: **AI may be the headline, but not always the real timeline.** While many layoffs are being presented in AI terms, Jensen Huang says generative AI only recently became productive at scale — so some cuts may be more about broader restructuring, cost pressure, or investor messaging than immediate AI replacement. At the same time, the *actual* AI impact on jobs may still be ahead.
